Naturicide

by Alice Denny, Emily O'Connell, Neal Haddaway, Olivia Pistun, and Zack West

Silenced song

With fading bird species comes the silencing of their song. This sound piece serves as a sonic interpretation of the alarming data showing our declining population of birds. As the song progresses, the calls of each bird are gradually removed, leaving our ears with a sense of what little remains today, and what is yet to come if their numbers continue to decline.

Fading feathers

Recall the childhood excitement of spotting a pristine feather on a countryside walk and adding it to a collection. Many of the birds to which these feathers belong are seeing their numbers decline, perhaps soon to be gone forever as a result, in part, of intensive agricultural pesticide use. Since 1980, the population of the 39 European bird species that live around farmland fell by over 50% - here, the feathers redden in line with data on European farmland population decline.

The graduating crimson hue of the feathers serves as a stark warning, providing a visual representation of the annual decline of bird numbers.
